# MATLAB Codes for Cycle-Free Homogeneous $d$-Partitions of the Complete Graph $K_6$

This repository contains the MATLAB codes developed in support of the results presented in this thesis. The codes are designed to investigate cycle-free homogeneous $d$-partitions of complete graphs, with particular emphasis on the case $d=3$, corresponding to the complete graph $K_6$.

The repository includes algorithms for generating both ordered and unordered cycle-free partitions of $K_6$, implementing the group action described by Lippold et al. (2022), and computing the associated groups $H_3$ and $G_3$. In addition, the repository contains scripts for comparing structural properties of these groups through the orders of their elements.

---

## Mathematical Background

The computations in this repository are based on the study of cycle-free homogeneous $d$-partitions of the complete graph $K_{2d}$. Throughout this work, we focus on the case

$$
d = 3,
$$

so that

$$
K_{2d} = K_6.
$$

A central result used throughout the computations is the following lemma due to Lippold et al. (2022).

### Lemma (Lippold et al., 2022)

Let

$$
(\Gamma_1, \ldots, \Gamma_d)
$$

be a cycle-free homogeneous $d$-partition of $K_{2d}$, and let $x,y,z$ be three distinct vertices of $K_{2d}$. Then there exists a unique cycle-free homogeneous $d$-partition

$$
(\Lambda_1, \ldots, \Lambda_d)
$$

such that the two partitions agree on every edge of $K_{2d}$ except on the face $(x,y,z)$, where they differ on at least two edges.

The MATLAB scripts implement this operation computationally and study the permutations induced by repeated applications of this group action.

# Running the Codes

Open MATLAB and run the desired script directly from the command window. For example,

```matlab
run('Listing1_UnorderedPartitions_K6.m')
```

The scripts may be executed independently; however, for ease of interpretation, it is recommended that they be explored in the order in which they appear in the appendices.

# Notes

* $K_6$ denotes the complete graph on six vertices.
* Since $K_6 = K_{2d}$ with $d=3$, every homogeneous partition consists of three classes.
* Each class is required to be cycle-free and therefore forms a spanning tree of $K_6$.
* The group $H_3$ arises from the action on unordered tree partitions.
* The group $G_3$ arises from the action on ordered tree partitions.
* The computations provide experimental evidence supporting the structural properties discussed throughout the thesis.
